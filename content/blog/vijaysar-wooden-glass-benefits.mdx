## What Is a Vijaysar Wooden Glass?

A Vijaysar wooden glass (also called a Vijaysar tumbler) is a traditional Indian drinking vessel handcrafted from the heartwood of the **Vijaysar tree** — one of the most revered medicinal trees in Ayurveda. The wood is dense, naturally fragrant, and rich in bioactive compounds.

The ritual is simple: fill the glass with water at night, let it sit for 6–8 hours, and drink the naturally infused water first thing in the morning on an empty stomach.

The water takes on a subtle pale pink or light brown tinge overnight — this is the natural tannins and botanical properties of the wood gently infusing into the water. It is completely safe and expected.

## How to Use a Vijaysar Wooden Glass Correctly

Getting the most from your Vijaysar glass is straightforward — the key is **consistency**:

1. **Fill at night** — pour room temperature drinking water into the glass. Do not use hot or boiled water.
2. **Leave overnight** — cover and let sit for 6–8 hours. This is when the wood infuses into the water.
3. **Drink in the morning** — on an empty stomach, ideally before tea or coffee.
4. **Rinse and dry** — rinse gently with plain water (no soap), dry completely, and it is ready for the next evening.

**What to expect:** The water will take on a pale pinkish or light brown colour. This is completely normal — it is the natural wood compounds. If you see no colour at all after several days, contact the seller as the wood may not be authentic Vijaysar.
