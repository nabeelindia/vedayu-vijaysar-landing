## What Is Vijaysar Wood?

**Vijaysar** (*Pterocarpus marsupium*), also called the Indian Kino Tree or Malabar Kino, is a large deciduous tree native to India and Sri Lanka. It grows in the dry forests of Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka, and the Western Ghats.

The heartwood of the Vijaysar tree is distinctive — dense, reddish-brown, and naturally aromatic. It has been a cornerstone of Ayurvedic medicine for over 2,000 years, mentioned in classical texts including the **Charaka Samhita** and **Ashtanga Hridayam**. For a broader overview of all the traditional benefits attributed to this wood, see our guide on [Vijaysar wooden glass benefits](/blog/vijaysar-wooden-glass-benefits).

In Ayurveda, Vijaysar is classified as *Tikta* (bitter) and *Kashaya* (astringent) in taste — qualities that Ayurvedic practitioners associate with balancing *Kapha* and *Pitta* doshas and supporting metabolic health.

## Vijaysar and Blood Sugar: What Ayurvedic Tradition Says

In Ayurvedic texts, Vijaysar (*Pterocarpus marsupium*) is one of the primary botanicals mentioned in the context of **Prameha** — a category of conditions in Ayurveda that broadly corresponds to metabolic and urinary disorders, including what modern medicine classifies as diabetes.

Classical Ayurvedic formulations using Vijaysar wood have been prescribed by practitioners for centuries as part of comprehensive wellness protocols for Prameha management.

The heartwood contains several naturally occurring phytochemicals that have attracted attention in botanical research, including:

- **Epicatechin** — a flavonoid also found in dark chocolate and green tea
- **Pterosupin and Marsupin** — naturally occurring chalcones unique to *Pterocarpus marsupium*
- **Pterostilbene** — a stilbenoid compound

These compounds are the focus of ongoing botanical and Ayurvedic research. However, it is important to understand the distinction between traditional use and clinical proof — see the disclaimer section below.

## How People Use the Vijaysar Glass for Blood Sugar Wellness

The traditional method is straightforward:

1. **Fill the Vijaysar wooden glass with room temperature water** — typically 150–200 ml
2. **Leave overnight** — for 6–8 hours (do not exceed 10 hours)
3. **Drink on an empty stomach** — first thing in the morning, before breakfast, tea, or medication
4. **Rinse and repeat** — rinse with plain water, dry thoroughly, refill that evening

The water will turn a pale pink or light brownish colour overnight — this is the natural phytochemicals from the wood infusing into the water. This is expected and completely safe. A glass that produces no colour change may not be authentic Vijaysar wood.

**Recommended cycle:** 90 days of daily use, followed by a 15–30 day break. Many people continue indefinitely after consulting their Ayurvedic practitioner.

## Can Vijaysar Glass Be Used Alongside Diabetes Medication?

Many people with Type 2 diabetes use the Vijaysar glass as part of their morning wellness routine *alongside* their prescribed medication — not as a replacement.

**If you are on diabetes medication**, please note:
- Always consult your doctor or diabetologist before adding any new element to your routine
- Continue your prescribed medication as directed — do not reduce or stop medication based on any natural product
- Monitor your blood sugar levels regularly as advised by your doctor
- Inform your doctor that you are using Vijaysar wood infused water

The Vijaysar glass is best thought of as a **complementary wellness habit** — like drinking warm lemon water, taking a morning walk, or reducing refined sugar — not as a medical treatment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does the Vijaysar glass work immediately?**
No. Like most Ayurvedic wellness practices, consistency over time is what matters. Traditional guidelines recommend 90 days of daily use.

**What if I forget to fill it one night?**
Just resume the next night. The practice is about building a long-term habit, not perfection.

**Can I use the Vijaysar glass if I have Type 1 diabetes?**
Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune condition that requires insulin therapy. Consult your doctor before introducing any complementary practice.

**Will the glass last a long time?**
With proper care (plain water rinse only, thorough drying, dry storage), a quality Vijaysar glass lasts for years. Do not use soap, detergent, or dishwashers.
